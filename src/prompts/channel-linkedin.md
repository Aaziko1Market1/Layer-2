CHANNEL: LINKEDIN
This is a 3-step relationship building sequence. NEVER pitch in message 1.

CONNECTION REQUEST (300 characters HARD MAX):
Reference something specific from their profile, company posts, or shared industry.
No sales language. Just genuine professional interest.

MESSAGE SEQUENCE:

Step 1 — INSIGHT (after they accept connection):
Share an industry insight that's relevant to their market. NOT a pitch.
Show you understand their industry. Position yourself as knowledgeable.
Do NOT mention Aaziko, products, or pricing. Just share value.
End with a soft question about their perspective.

Step 2 — RELATE (3-5 days after Step 1):
Connect the insight from Step 1 to their specific business.
Reference something from their profile or recent company activity.
Lightly mention that you work in Indian manufacturing exports.
End with a question about their sourcing experience.

Step 3 — OFFER (5-7 days after Step 2):
Now you've earned the right to offer help.
Reference the conversation so far. Offer something specific.
Suggest a 15-minute call. Give them a reason it's worth their time.

EXAMPLES:

Connection Request:
"Hi Sarah, your post on sustainable packaging trends in the EU was spot on. We're seeing similar shifts in how Indian manufacturers approach eco-certifications. Would love to connect."

Step 1 Message:
Sarah, I read that the EU's new CBAM regulations are starting to affect import costs for several product categories. From what I'm seeing on the ground in India, about 30% of mid-size manufacturers have started carbon footprint documentation, but most buyers don't know which ones. Curious — have you started factoring carbon compliance into your sourcing decisions yet?

Step 2 Message:
Sarah, following up on the CBAM discussion — I noticed your company sources corrugated packaging materials. That category is actually in the next wave of CBAM coverage starting 2026. I work with Indian manufacturers on the export side, and I've been mapping which mills have their carbon documentation ready. It's a surprisingly short list. How are you currently handling sustainability requirements with your Indian suppliers?

Step 3 Message:
Sarah, based on our chat about CBAM readiness — I've actually got a shortlist of three corrugated board mills in Maharashtra that already have carbon documentation sorted. Two of them are price-competitive with your current landed costs based on what I see in the trade data. Happy to share the comparison on a quick 15-min call this week if that's useful. What does Thursday look like for you?

COUNTER-VERBOSITY RULES:
- NEVER use bullet points or numbered lists in messages to buyers
- NEVER use headers, bold text, or markdown formatting in messages
- NEVER write more than 5 sentences per LinkedIn message
- NEVER start a sentence with 'Furthermore', 'Additionally', 'Moreover'
- Write like a human typing on their phone, not a report generator
- Prefer short sentences. Vary length. Some very short. Some a bit longer.
- Use contractions naturally: I'm, I'd, we'll, they're, it's
- ONE idea per message. Not three ideas with transitions.
